The orchestra

Six phases. Twenty-five steps. One orchestrated flow.

The current-state process runs 25 discrete steps across 6 phases — Request Intake, Validation & Engineering, Customer Master Data & Financial Setup, Quote/Contract/Payment, Construction, and Meter Installation / Closeout. Each phase is reimagined as a Lean Core process, a Hybrid workflow, or an autonomous AI agent, preserving the SAP S/4 and Salesforce systems of record. Humans are retained where judgement, liability, or authorisation genuinely requires them: the PE stamp (design-required cases), the internal quote approval, the WBSe approval for new scope, the dispatch supervisor release, and the crew safety sign-off.

Design-from-Zero Agent (design-required subset)

Applies only to the subset of cases that require engineering design — typically ~40% of residential volume and most commercial. Drafts load calcs, conductor sizing, transformer placement, route optimisation against the GIS network model, and materials BOM in under 30 min. A credentialed PE conducts independent structured review and issues formal stamp with attestation — typically within 2–3 days vs. 9-day legacy cycle. PE liability is unchanged. Blanket-WBSe cases skip this step entirely.

A note on case mix. Not every connection traverses every step. Roughly ~40% of residential requests require engineering design (and therefore the Design-from-Zero + PE stamp path); the remainder use a blanket pre-existing WBSe and skip the design-track entirely. Commercial requests almost always require design, and loads above the state PUC threshold additionally trigger the parallel DSIS regulatory track. Cycle-time claims in this document refer to the design-required residential case unless otherwise stated; blanket-WBSe cases are already faster today and offer less compression.

The program does not reduce field-execution time. Linemen, trenching, conductor pull, and meter set are paced by crew availability and physical work. The 28 days recovered are entirely from queue time, handoff latency, and document preparation — i.e., work that adds no customer value today.
